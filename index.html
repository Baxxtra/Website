About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher and Lecturer at the Technical University of Munich (TUM), with a research focus on sustainable and climate finance. My work explores how financial markets assess corporate climate and biodiversity risks. I use text analysis, machine learning, and financial econometrics to study risk pricing. In 2024, I was awarded the ECB Lamfalussy Fellowship for my research on biodiversity risk in syndicated lending.

Research

My research lies at the intersection of climate risk, corporate finance, and data science. I co-founded the Aggregate Confusion Project, and currently work on firm-level climate exposure, ESG rating divergence, and the role of biodiversity in financial markets. I collaborate with scholars from MIT Sloan and the ECB, combining rigorous empirical methods with modern NLP.
